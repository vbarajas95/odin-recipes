About

Lasagna is considered one of the oldest types of pasta that is made of flat, wide sheets. According to historians, lasagna originated during the Middle Ages in Italy. While the variations of lasagna vary among regions, the dish primarily consists of ingredients such as vegetables, cheeses, seasonings and spices.

Ingredients

  • lasagna noodles
  • tomato-basil pasta sauce
  • ground beef
  • Italian pork sausage
  • ricotta cheese
  • shredded mozzarella cheese
  • hot water

Steps

  1. Cook ground beef and Italian pork sausage in a large skillet over medium heat, stirring until fully cooked; drain. Add pasta sauce.
  2. In a lightly greased baking dish, spread 1/3 of meat sauce and layer pasta noodles and half of mozzarella cheese and ricotta cheese. Repeat and spread remaining sauce over mozzarella cheese. Add hot water around the edge of the dish. Cover baking dish with at least two layers of aluminum foil.
  3. Bake dish at 375 degrees Fahrenheit for 45 minutes; uncover and bake for at least 10 minutes.
  4. Let dish stand for 10 minutes before serving.
